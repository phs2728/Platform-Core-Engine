# Client Interview Plan — CP-001 Discovery Conversation

> **Purpose**: Surface the client's goals, team capabilities, content readiness, and constraints in a single Discovery Conversation. The conversation is the **only artifact the client sees** from this phase.

---

## 1. Conversation framing (what the client sees)

**Subject line of the meeting**: "Discovery Conversation: Envoy Hostel & Tours — Goals + Workflow + Constraints"

**One-sentence opener**: "Thank you for inviting us. We have ~15 questions that help us shape the right website for your team. Please answer what's most useful; we'll fill gaps from our experience."

**One-sentence closer**: "We'll send you a Proposal within 5 business days. Anything else you'd like us to know?"

---

## 2. Questions (15; ~5 minutes each)

| # | Question | Why we ask | Internal map |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | "Who are your most common guests? Walk us through 1-2 recent guest stories — what did they want, what made them happy, what made them hesitate or complain?" | Persona refresh | Customer Discovery Report |
| 2 | "Of bookings in the last 30 days, what % came direct (your own channels) vs. OTAs (Booking.com, Hostelworld)?" | Direct-vs-OTA mix baseline; informs Trust evidence targets | Trust Evidence + Success Metrics |
| 3 | "What 3 things, if we fixed them on your website, would most increase your direct bookings?" | Identify hypothesis priorities | Hypothesis prioritization |
| 4 | "Walk us through what happens when a guest arrives. Who greets them? What time? What paperwork or check-in takes longest?" | Operational context — informs UX flow + chatbot | UX Research + Content Strategy |
| 5 | "Walk us through what happens when a guest has a problem or complaint." | Hotel ops context — informs AI chatbot scope and admin alerts | UX + Backend |
| 6 | "What's your current payment provider? Any fees or restrictions we should know about?" | Payment integration scope | Backend |
| 7 | "What languages do you commonly serve? Which one matters most for the next 12 months?" | i18n prioritization | Frontend |
| 8 | "Do you have a brand book, logo, or visual references? If not, are you open to our designer proposing a visual direction?" | Brand DNA scope | Visual Design |
| 9 | "Is your team comfortable updating room prices and descriptions via a CMS (no coding required)?" | CMS scope | CMS Configuration |
| 10 | "What's your team's comfort with running A/B tests and interpreting results? Or do you want us to handle that entirely?" | Hypothesis testing transparency | Experiment Plan |
| 11 | "What legal or regulatory requirements exist for Tbilisi hostels (tourism license, GDPR-like rules, age restrictions)?" | Compliance scope | QES |
| 12 | "What's your ideal daily or weekly cadence for check-ins with us during the build?" | Project management | PM |
| 13 | "Are there past website efforts you've tried that didn't work? Why?" | Anti-patterns to avoid | UX + Design |
| 14 | "If this engagement succeeds wildly in 6 months, what does that look like for your hostel?" | Outcomes = success metric | Success Metrics |
| 15 | "Anything else we should know that we haven't asked about?" | Open feedback | All |

---

## 3. Deliverables from this conversation

After the conversation, the Platform Agency produces:

1. **Internal** synthesis: persona refresh, hypothesis prioritization, scope refinements.
2. **Client-facing** outputs (these the client sees):
   - Discovery Conversation Summary (a 1-page brief — not this plan, just a friendly summary).
   - Proposal (linking to the Brief and Pricing section).
   - Updated Design Direction (if conversation reveals a visual change).

---

## 4. Anti-patterns

| Anti-pattern | Why forbidden |
|---|---|
| Ask the client "do you want Engine X or Engine Y?" | Client doesn't know what Engines are |
| Ask the client to "approve the PRD" | Client doesn't know what PRD is |
| Show the client our hypothesis IDs | Client doesn't need them |
| Say "we're testing variant A vs. variant B" | Client sees "we tested and chose what works" |

The client's experience is: **a senior agency team asks thoughtful questions, then delivers a beautiful, functional website**. That's the whole experience.
