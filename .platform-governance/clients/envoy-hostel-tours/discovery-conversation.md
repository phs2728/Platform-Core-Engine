# Discovery Conversation — initial framing for the client

> **Plain language version** of the Discovery Conversation we run with the client. This is what the client sees.

---

## 1. What this conversation is

A 60-90 minute meeting between the Envoy team and our Platform Agency team. We'll cover:

- Your guests and their needs.
- Your current operations and what you'd like preserved or improved.
- Your technical environment (payment provider, booking engine).
- Your team capabilities and content readiness.
- Your goals for the next 12 months.

## 2. What we'll do with this information

We'll use your answers to:

- Propose a website design that fits your guests' style.
- Propose a content/CMS structure that your team can manage comfortably.
- Propose the right scope for AI chatbot and admin tools.
- Propose a build plan with realistic timeline.
- Send you a Proposal within 5 business days.

## 3. What we **won't** do

We won't ask you to:

- Approve any technical specifications beyond what's reasonable for a hospitality business.
- Make decisions about internal tools or methods you've never used.
- Pay for hidden fees beyond what's clearly in the Brief.

---

## 4. Question preview (so you can prepare)

We'll ask ~15 questions across 5 themes:

1. **Your guests** — who they are, what they want, what they complain about.
2. **Your operations** — what happens on arrival, checkout, problem-handling, etc.
3. **Your content** — brand assets, photos, written descriptions.
4. **Your technical environment** — payment provider, existing tools.
5. **Your goals** — what success looks like in 6 months.

We sent the full question list a few days before the meeting so you can prepare.

---

## 5. After the conversation

You receive:

- A 1-page summary of what we heard.
- A Proposal (within 5 business days).
- A timeline for the project.
- A clear list of what we'll deliver.

You don't receive:

- Engineering specifications.
- Internal QA reports.
- Internal team meeting notes.

That's just our work — not yours.
