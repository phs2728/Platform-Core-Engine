# Why we're priced at $10,000+ — and why that saves you money

> **For Envoy Hostel & Tours** — a brief on what $10K+ gets you, what it would cost you to do it differently, and how the engagement is structured.

---

## 1. The benchmark

A premium hospitality website, hand-built by an agency you can name, with a senior designer, a senior engineer, a project manager, a content lead, and a QA lead — typically runs **$15,000 – $50,000** in the US/Europe.

In Tbilisi's market, a similar scope from a traditional agency is **₾25,000 – ₾40,000** ($9,000 – $15,000), but typically with thinner ongoing ownership.

We're priced at **US$10,000+** (or equivalent in GEL), which puts us **at or below** the regional agency benchmark while delivering:

- A premium-agency quality design.
- A production-grade booking flow.
- A CMS your team can use.
- An AI chatbot.
- Multilingual support (en, ko, ka).
- Performance + accessibility + security reviews.
- 14 days of post-launch support.

## 3. What you can expect at this price

**Premium design**: A clean, restrained, photo-forward design — the kind of restraint you'd see in boutique hotels and editorial travel magazines. **Not** template-driven; not generic.

**Real performance**: Sub-2.5s Largest Contentful Paint on a mid-range mobile on 4G. Lighthouse mobile ≥ 85. Faster than most hostels' websites.

**Real booking conversion**: Booking flow designed from traveler behavior — Trust Stages (Anxiety → Discovery → Evaluation → Confidence → Action), where each stage has a UI affordance to reduce hesitation.

**Easy management**: Your team can update rooms, prices, photos, FAQs without writing code or hiring a developer.

**AI chatbot** that handles 5 of the 10 most common questions (check-in time, luggage storage, neighborhood, breakfast, cancellation policy) so your team spends less time on repetitive questions.

**International reach**: English, Korean, Georgian — three of your most likely guest languages.

**Multilingual SEO**: Travelers searching in their language find you.

**Accessibility**: WCAG 2.1 AA — usable by travelers with disabilities.
