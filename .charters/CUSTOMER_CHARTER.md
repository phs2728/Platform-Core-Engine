## 3. Operating interpretation (for Platform Owners, Engineers, and AI Agents)

This section is **not** part of the Charter text. It is a non-normative operating note recording the Council Chair's published rationale alongside PCR-003. The Charter's binding authority is §1 and §2 only.

### 3.1 What the Customer sees

Per §2 verbatim, **the client-facing surface is the deliverable**:

- Beautiful design (사장님 확립 design 헌장: Kinfolk/Aman-grade, Pretendard, qvevri-wine, no serif, no AI gradient)
- Fast speed
- Booking-ready structure
- Easy-to-manage CMS
- AI chatbot
- SEO
- Admin pages
- Mobile experience

These are the **8 client-visible outputs** defined in PCR-003.

### 3.2 What the Customer does NOT see

Per §2 verbatim, **the customer must never need to understand**:

- PRD, TRD
- Agency OS
- QES, Skills, Playbooks
- Platform internals

These are **internal implementation details**. They are present in the Platform but never surface to the customer.

### 3.3 Why Platform-internal mechanics remain

The Platform's internal mechanics — Discovery Engine, PRD Engine, TRD Engine, Agency OS, Skill Library, Playbooks, QES, PAG, Knowledge Governance, EPS, C-24 — remain in force **for the Platform team only**. They are how the Platform Agency achieves its $10k+ premium output. They are the means, not the message.

### 3.4 C-24 confirmation

This Charter **reinforces** C-24 (Constitution §15): no new Engines/Standards/Playbooks are added during Client Project execution. The Platform's frozen v1.1 / v1.2 is the only substrate.

### 3.5 Customer-centric re-orientation

The Council Chair notes that prior reasoning was "Platform 중심". Future reasoning must be "고객 중심". This is a **re-orientation**, not a redesign — the Platform internals remain identical, only the orientation of effort changes.
